# Claim-package export and keyboard-readiness verification — July 23, 2026

Scope: fictional-data Alpha only  
Branch tested: `codex/ops-export-keyboard-audit`  
Deployment performed: No

## Outcome

The independent portions of TEST-003 and TEST-004 were completed locally. The full signed-out 11-step claim flow produced a grounded fictional statement, required all six statement sections to be confirmed, enabled the condition-review export, returned a valid PDF response, and displayed a live download-completion message.

The review also found and corrected two accessibility and reliability defects:

1. Questionnaire step changes left focus on the previous action instead of moving it to the newly displayed question content.
2. The generated PDF link was clicked without being attached to the document and its Blob URL was revoked immediately, while completion had no assistive-technology announcement.

The question region now receives visible programmatic focus after a step change. The PDF control now uses an attached, named download element, delays Blob revocation, announces errors as alerts, and announces successful initiation through a polite status message.

Opening the downloaded file from a representative physical device therefore remains a human TEST-003 check and is not represented as independently complete.

## Keyboard and accessibility evidence

- Step activation now moves focus to a labeled region such as “Step 2 of 11: Claim path.”
- The focused question region has a visible three-pixel outline.
- Sixteen public or signed-out routes were checked at 390 × 844 and 1280 × 900.
- Every route had exactly one level-one heading.
- No checked route had horizontal overflow.
- No visible interactive control lacked a detectable accessible name.
- No visible control used a positive `tabindex`.
- Protected Document Upload, Claim Package, and Account routes redirected signed-out users to Login at both sizes.
- Existing mobile navigation focus/inert, progress semantics, reduced motion, touch-target, error recovery, and distinct-title contracts remain green.

The available browser-control layer did not produce a reliable Tab or Enter focus trail, consistent with the earlier Alpha report. A human keyboard-only and VoiceOver pass therefore remains required before TEST-004 can be closed.
